Swap broccoli for brussel sprouts

Learn how to replace broccoli with brussel sprouts in your favorite recipes, and where to find fresh brussel sprouts locally.

Roasted Garlic Parmesan brussel sprouts

Anywhere you would roast broccoli, you can roast brussel sprouts instead.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups halved brussel sprouts
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss brussel sprouts with olive oil, garlic, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a baking sheet and roast 20–25 minutes until edges are browned and crisp.
  4. Sprinkle with Parmesan and roast 3–5 more minutes until melted.

Quick Sesame Stir-Fried brussel sprouts

If your stir-fry usually uses broccoli, swap in thinly sliced brussel sprouts for a softer bite and slightly sweeter flavor.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups thinly sliced brussel sprouts
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

Directions

  1. Heat sesame oil in a skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add brussel sprouts and cook 4–6 minutes, stirring often.
  3. Stir in soy sauce and honey; cook 1–2 more minutes.
  4. Top with sesame seeds and serve over rice or noodles.

Shaved brussel sprouts Salad

Instead of a crunchy broccoli slaw, use shredded brussel sprouts for a tender, leafy texture.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups finely shredded brussel sprouts
  • 1/3 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup chopped almonds or pecans
  • 1/4 cup grated hard cheese (Parmesan or similar)
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Directions

  1. Whisk olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon, salt, and pepper in a small bowl.
  2. In a large bowl, combine shredded brussel sprouts, cranberries, nuts, and cheese.
  3. Pour dressing over the salad and toss well.
  4. Let sit 10–15 minutes to soften the brussel sprouts before serving.

Find Fresh brussel sprouts

For local, seasonal brussel sprouts, visit the Provo Farmers Market at Pioneer Park. You can often find vendors selling both brussel sprouts and broccoli straight from nearby farms.
